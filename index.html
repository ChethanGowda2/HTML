Safari Experience

Visitors can enjoy bus and jeep safaris to watch animals in their natural habitat.It is one of the most exciting activities in the park.Lions are one of the biggest attractions in the park. Visitors can watch them closely during the safari ride. The Safari Section is the most sought out by visitors, from within and outside India. The Safari unit provides a guided observation of captive wildlife within large-scale, immersive exhibits designed to replicate their natural forest ecosystems. The Safari region's landscape is a representation of the adjoining dry and moist deciduous forests of the Bannerghatta National Park, which is also home to a wide variety of fauna which are often spotted within BBP safari range as well. The safari is further subdivided into different sections and visitors get to learn and explore the safari area in a protected and closed buses and jeeps which are provided by the zoo, in partnership with the Karnataka State Tourism Development Cooperation (KSTDC). They usually rest in groups and sometimes walk near the vehicles.

ZOO PREPAREDNESS INSTRUCTIONS

1. Wear clothing that merge with nature. Avoid wearing florescent/bright colour clothing.
2. Wear comfortable shoes, carry a backpack, water bottle, hat, headphone (BBP is a silent zone) , raincoat, etc. You may have to walk about 2 to 3 km.
3. Safari tickets are limited in number.
4. Facilities:- Cloak room, wheelchairs, Toilets, resting places, hand sanitizers.
5. Book tickets online.
6. Single use plastic covers/ items are not permitted inside the zoo.
7. Any liquids and essential food items should be securely contained in a multiuse container in the backpack in order to avoid snatching by free ranging monkeys.
8. We encourage you to carry back waste generated by you and dispose it within BBMP limits.
9. Wish everyone an enriching, educative experience in the Park!.

Interesting Facts about our zoo

Gayatri, an 87-year-old zoo elephant is a resident at the Bannerghatta Biological Park in Bengaluru. With her advanced age, she faces many problems that most senior citizens face.Her eyesight fails her and her hearing is not what it used to be but yet, she looks forward to a romp in the forest at night.
